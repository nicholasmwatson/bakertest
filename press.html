Approved boilerplate · 200 words

Baker Industries is a Philadelphia-area nonprofit that has provided paid transitional employment to adults with barriers to work since 1980. Founded by Charlie and Weezie Baker to give their son Justin — who has a disability — a chance at real work, Baker is distinctive for serving four populations together: adults with disabilities, returning citizens, people in recovery, and adults experiencing housing insecurity — side by side on one integrated production floor.

Participants earn a paycheck from day one on real production lines — kitting, packaging, shrink-wrapping, mailing, and light assembly — while receiving wraparound coaching, job readiness workshops, and pathways to permanent employment. Baker operates facilities in Malvern and Philadelphia, with a third facility opening in Norristown in 2026. In 2025, Baker served more than 200 participants and placed 60 graduates into competitive, living-wage employment — while running on zero government funding in 45+ years of operation. Roughly half of Baker's revenue comes from earned production contracts; the rest from local philanthropy.
