The Job Readiness Program

Twelve weeks. Paid from day one. Real work on real client orders — plus weekly workshops and one-on-one coaching that together get participants into living-wage jobs.

Participants arrive Monday. By Wednesday they're on a fulfillment line, assembling, packaging, or shipping product for clients across the Delaware Valley. That's not busywork — it's the actual work Baker gets paid to do. The program is intentionally designed this way: learning is faster when the stakes are real.

Do you qualify?

Baker serves adults who face barriers to employment. You may be a good fit if you identify with any of the following:

✓Returning from incarceration or currently on parole / probation
✓Living with a physical, intellectual, or developmental disability
✓In recovery from substance use disorder
✓Currently experiencing or recently exited homelessness
✓Referred by a social service or reentry organization
✓Ready to work and willing to commit to 12 weeks

Program · 02

Long-term employment

For participants — often those with significant disabilities — who thrive at Baker and are not transitioning to outside employment, Baker is the job.

Some of our team members have been here for decades. Justin Baker, for whom this organization was founded, has worked here for 45 years. For these individuals, Baker provides not just income but community, structure, and purpose that long-term employment uniquely delivers.

This is not a consolation track. Many long-term employees are among Baker's most skilled and reliable workers — the institutional knowledge that keeps production running smoothly every single day.

Program · 03

Wraparound support

Showing up to work is impossible when the rest of your life is unstable. We address that.

Baker partners with local social service organizations to connect participants with housing assistance, mental health services, legal aid, and financial counseling. Our coaches don't just track attendance — they know what's happening outside of Baker and help navigate it.

We believe employment is most durable when it's supported. Participants who get help with housing and healthcare are more likely to stay employed after placement — and less likely to cycle back into the systems they escaped.

Who we serve

Four populations.
One team. Side by side.

⚖️

Returning citizens

Adults reentering after incarceration, navigating parole, court dates, and a world that has changed. Baker provides structure, real wages, and a record of employment that opens the next door.

♿

Adults with disabilities

Physical, intellectual, and developmental. Many have been part of Baker's community for decades. Some work here for life — alongside participants in transition, on the same line, doing the same work.

🌿

Adults in recovery

From substance use disorder — stability, routine, and paid work form the foundation for sustained recovery. Baker doesn't require perfection; it requires showing up.

🏠

Experiencing homelessness

Meeting people where they are, with wraparound services to stabilize housing alongside employment. Income is the first step. Baker makes it accessible.

Baker is one of the only organizations in the region to blend these four populations into a single, integrated, high-performing team — not siloed programs.
