Featured story · Nate

Twenty-nine years inside.
Six months at Baker.
One trade, for life.

Nate came home after 29 years in prison. He didn't know how to use a debit card. A counselor at the halfway house told him about a place on I Street. He walked in, and started work that week.

Through Baker's Job Readiness Program he learned packaging, assembly, and warehouse work. He ran the Tastykake line. Six months later — half the typical program length — Baker placed him with a high-end Philadelphia manufacturer. Today, pressing is his primary role. Baker didn't rescue Nate. It put him to work.

The model that makes
this possible.

~50%
Earned revenue

Real production contracts from real businesses. Tastykake, GSK, Crazy Aaron's, and 75+ more regional partners pay us for contract manufacturing, packaging, and fulfillment. This revenue creates the accountability and dignity that charity alone can't.

~50%
Local philanthropy

Foundations, individuals, and events — $1.2M raised in 2024. This side funds what earned revenue can't: case management, coaching, and the patience to let people progress at their own pace. Local funders, close to the problem, free of federal compliance overhead.

The case for local, independent funding

"In 45+ years, Baker has never accepted a dollar of federal funding. That's a design choice, not a fallback — and it's why the model still works."

84% of nonprofits with government funding expect cuts. 40% of human-services grantees report government grants as their primary revenue stream. Baker was never in that position. Your philanthropic dollar isn't backfill — it's the whole architecture.

The policy case
52%
National recidivism rate

The rate at which returning citizens are re-incarcerated within three years. It's not a character problem — it's a structural one. Reentry without employment isn't reentry.

16%
With stable post-release employment

The recidivism rate drops to roughly 16% for individuals who secure stable employment after release. Work itself is the intervention. Not counseling alone. Not housing alone. Work.

For people looking for work

Who we work with.
And how it works.

Baker works with four populations together on the same floor — because we believe that integration is part of what makes the model strong.

⚖️
Returning citizens

Whether you're coming home from a short sentence or a long one, Baker's structure is designed for reentry. Parole check-ins, court dates, and transition needs are expected here — not frowned upon.

🌱
People in recovery

Baker provides a stable, structured environment that supports recovery. You'll have real work to come back to each day — and a team that wants you to succeed.

♿
Adults with disabilities

Baker was founded on this mission — our founder Charlie Baker designed the program so his son Justin could have a real job. That commitment has never changed.

🏠
People experiencing housing insecurity

Stable employment is one of the most direct paths to stable housing. Baker is often an early step in that journey — a paycheck from day one, and a structure to build from.

What to expect
Step 01
You start work. You get paid.

From day one, you're working on a real production floor doing real work — packaging, assembly, fulfillment, manufacturing. And you're earning a real paycheck for it. No waiting period, no probation.

Step 02
You build skills for the long run.

Baker's Job Readiness Program runs alongside your time on the floor. Resume writing, financial management, workplace leadership, and technology skills — all in a trauma-informed environment built around where you actually are.

Step 03
Baker helps you make the next move.

When you're ready, Baker helps connect you with permanent, competitive employment — with regional employers who know Baker participants are among the best workers they've ever hired.
