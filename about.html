Our purpose

Every individual deserves meaningful work.

Mission
Employ those who have the most difficulty getting and holding jobs — people facing barriers that most employers won't look past.
Teach the work ethic through real, paid work experience on actual client orders — not simulated tasks or unpaid internships.
Act as a critical transition step toward permanent employment for those ready to move on — and a permanent home for those who thrive here.
Help individuals reach their highest potential — through coaching, workshops, wraparound support, and a team that refuses to give up on them.

History · 46 years

Four chapters.
One mission.

1980
Founded
Charlie and Weezie Baker start Baker Industries in their Chester County garage to create meaningful work for their son Justin — and for others facing employment barriers. Justin is still here, 45 years later.
1990s
The model expands
Baker broadens its mission beyond adults with disabilities to include returning citizens, adults in recovery from substance use disorder, and people experiencing homelessness — all working side by side on one integrated, high-performing team.
2015
Philadelphia opens
Baker opens its Kensington location, meeting participants where the need is greatest. The Philadelphia facility serves adults reentering from incarceration and those in early recovery — right in one of the region's hardest-hit communities.
2024
GSK IMPACT Award
Baker receives the GSK IMPACT Award — national recognition of the Baker model and its measurable community outcomes. The same year, 60 participants are placed in competitive employment. Another 132 continue in long-term supported roles.
2025
Charlie turns 100
Founder Charlie Baker reaches his hundredth birthday. WHYY, 6ABC, and CBS3 cover the milestone. "Positive Pete" becomes a regional story. And Baker places graduates at an average starting wage of $19/hr — proof the model works at scale.
2026
Norristown opens
A third production facility comes online in Norristown in Spring 2026 — expanding Baker's reach into Montgomery County and creating capacity for 200 or more participants for the first time in the organization's history.
What we believe

The principles behind everything Baker does.

01
Real work, real pay
Participants aren't volunteers. They're not in a simulation. They're employees doing real work for real clients, earning real wages from their first week. Dignity starts there.
02
Integration, not isolation
Adults with disabilities work alongside returning citizens. Participants in recovery work beside long-term employees. One team. No separate tracks. Difference is the model — not the exception.
03
The whole person
A job doesn't fix everything. Baker wraps employment in coaching, counseling, housing referrals, and support systems because we know that showing up to work is hard when the rest of life is falling apart.
04
Fair chance hiring
Background checks and conviction histories are not disqualifiers here. We believe the justice system's debt has been paid and that every person deserves a fair shot at economic participation.
05
Earned, not given
Half of Baker's budget comes from client revenue. The work Baker participants do is real, valuable, and in-demand. That's not charity — it's commerce. And that distinction matters to everyone who works here.
06
Long view
Baker has been at this for 46 years. Justin Baker, for whom this organization was founded, still comes to work. The long view is how we measure success — not 90-day retention rates.
